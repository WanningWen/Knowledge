Thought about these: 
- "the rush to divorce makes no allowance for error, for human fragility. It also makes no allowance for repair, resilience, and recovery" (16)
and 
- "the problem with the judgmental, highly charged and repressive conversation about infidelity is that it precludes any possibility for deeper understanding, and therefore for hope and healing -- together or apart" (16)

These quotes bring me back to my discussion with [[Bella He]] and [[Lucy Wong|Lucy]] about whether girls from LFA's views on whether they should leave after their partner has cheated is related to their family dynamics of if their mothers were working/ had the ability to be independent or not.
- Lucy pointed out the nuances of how the mother's personalities are different:
	- ex: we'd think that [[Christina Bai Anhe|Christina]]'s wishful thinking of having a man provide for her for "nothing" in return is a result from her mother being a housewife
		- but we neglect the fact that Lucy has met [[Christina Bai Anhe|Christina]]'s mother; I have spoken to her, and she is not a soft spoken, traditional housewife by any means
I realized that maybe Bella's jumping to the conclusion -- of almost "shaming" women who would still stay after discovering infidelity as a sign of erosion of patriarchy and a instinctual disbelief that women can survive on their own-- is maybe precisely this "judgement. Maybe on the contrary it's her weakness to not belief people have the resilience to repair their relationship?
