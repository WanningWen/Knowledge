> [!important] particular patterns of illogical thinking 
> Identified by the [[Cognitive Behavioral Model|Cognitive Behavioral Theory]]

## Examples
### catastrophic thinking / catastrophizing
- characterized by a person always anticipating a terrible outcome, the worst possible outcome for all of their challenges.
Impacts:
- Cognitive behavioral theorists believed this thought pattern leads to significant struggles with [[anxiety]]
### all-or-nothing thinking 
 occurs when a person believes that everything is wonderful or often that everything is terrible and there's no in-between. 
 - people who employ all-or-nothing thinking have a strong tendency to become overwhelmed and feel helpless and hopeless which often leads to [[depression]]
### Magnification and Minimization
Exaggerating or minimizing the importance of events
### Disqualifying the Positive
Recognizing only the negative aspects of a situation while ignoring the positive
### Overgeneralization:
Whenever we go to the grocery store, he _always_ comments about how _whatever_ line he chooses, at _every_ store, it is always the slowest line. Does this happen _every_ time he is at the store? I’m doubtful, but his error in thinking leads to him believing this is true.
### Disconfirmation bias
- associated with [[Obsessive Compulsive Disorder|OCD]]
