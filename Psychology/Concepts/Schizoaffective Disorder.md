> [!important] [[psychosis|psychotic]] symptoms +  concurrent uninterrupted period of a major mood episode (major depressive or manic episode)
## Clinical Presentation
- requires the presence of a mood episode for the majority, if not the total duration of the disorder
	- [[anhedonia]] is common for schizophrenia, so only a pervasive depressed mood (APA, 2022) suffice
- psychotic symptoms should continue for at least two weeks in the absence of a major mood disorder (APA, 2022). 
	- difference between schizoaffective disorder and [[Major Depressive Disorder]] with psychotic features
