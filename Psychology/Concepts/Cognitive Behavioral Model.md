> [!important] focuses on thoughts or cognitions as well as behaviors of people who are struggling with psychological disorders. 
> Can be empirically researched and tested unlike the [[Psychodynamic model]], research helped understand [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] and [[Anxiety Disorders|Anxiety]]
## History
The cognitive behavioral model got started in the 1950s. At that time, psychoanalysis had been around for several decades and some scientists were beginning to grow impatient with its lack of scientific support. [[Psychoanalytic theory]] cannot be tested in a controlled research setting.

Followed experiments of animal learning scientists and conceptualized psychological abnormality entirely in behavioral terms. Then they realized that psychological abnormalities are more than maladaptive behaviors

 Then they started to look into [[cognitive processes]] or thought patterns and the role they play in the development of psychological abnormality
### Aaron Beck and Albert Ellis
Albert Ellis [[Rational-Emotive therapy]] to treat [[Anxiety Disorders|Anxiety]]  
## Elements:

> [!important] cognitive behavioral therapists do not employ an idea of the unconscious mind as [[Psychodynamic model|psychodynamic]] therapists do
> assert that thought patterns that produce psychological disorders (**automatic thought**) are so ingrained and habitual that people do not notice them.

## Treatment:
Treatment for psychological disorders should help people discover their [[cognitive distortions]] and come to understand the ways in which these thought patterns are impacting their emotions and behaviors, leading to psychological disorders
