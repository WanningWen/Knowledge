> [!important] [[Mental Health Treatment]] to help people to slow down and really examine and question [[automatic thoughts]]
> based on the [[Cognitive Behavioral Model]] useful for [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] and [[Anxiety Disorders|Anxiety]]:
## History
founded by Aaron Beck in the 1960s
- one of the first: [[Rational-Emotive therapy]] 
## Variations:
More recent variations seek to help clients ==identify and accept their [[cognitive distortions]]== rather than identify and *change* them.

## Methodology:
![[cognitive triangle]]
 ==identify and restructure maladaptive thoughts== while also providing opportunities to utilize these more effective thought patterns through ==exposure-based experiences==
#### cognitive interventions
- monitoring and recording
- identifying cognitive errors
- examining evidence supporting/negating cognitions
- creating rational alternatives to maladaptive thought patterns
#### Behavioral interventions
- activity planning
- pleasant event scheduling
- task assignments
- coping-skills training
### Four phases of treatment for [[Depressive Disorders|depression]]
#### Phase 1: Increasing pleasurable activities.
Similar to [[Behavioral activation|behavioral activation]], the clinician encourages the patient to ==identify and engage in activities that are pleasurable to the individual.==
- help the patient to select the activity
- plan when they will engage in that activity
#### Phase 2: Challenging [[automatic thoughts]]
 clinician provides ==psycho-education about the negative automatic thoughts== that can maintain depressive symptoms. 
 The patient will learn to ==identify these thoughts on their own== during the week and ==maintain a thought journal== of these cognitions to review with the clinician in session.
- [[self monitoring]]
- [[cognitive restructuring]]
#### Phase 3: Identifying negative thoughts. 
the clinician can help the patient ==identify _how_== these thoughts are maintaining their depressive symptoms.
- the patient begins to have direct insight as to how their cognitions contribute to their disorder.
#### Phase 4: Changing thoughts. 
challenging the negative thoughts the patient has been identifying in the last two phases of treatment and replacing them with positive thoughts.
### five components of CBT for [[Panic Disorder]] (Craske & Barlow, 2014)
#### Psycho education
correct misconceptions about panic symptoms 
- educating on nature of [[Panic Disorder]] and underlying causes
- mechanisms to maintain such as physical, cognitive, behavioral
#### [[self monitoring]]
self observation is essential
- patient taught to identify physiological cues leading up to and during [[panic attack]]
- recognize and document thoughts and behaviors associated with physiological symptoms
- relationship between physical arousal and cognitive behavioral response
- manage panic symptoms
#### relaxation training
- recent research says **breathing is not effective** in [[Panic Disorder]] due to incidence of hyperventilation during [[panic attack]]s (Schmidt et al., 2000)
- breathing retraining misused, more effective as physiological response to stress (Craske & Barlow, 2014)
