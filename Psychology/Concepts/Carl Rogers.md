> [!important] Psychologist known for developing [[person centered therapy]] and the term [[unconditional positive regard]] 
> Rogers believed that all people must receive [[unconditional positive regard]] from early important people in life, most often these are parents or other primary caregivers.
## Theory:
### [[unconditional positive regard]] creates a major barrier to adult development and prevents [[self actualization]]
  - when babies and young children ==receive unconditional positive regard== from their caregivers, they develop ==unconditional self regard== (The total and unconditional acceptance of themselves)
	  - this allows them to self actualize, to reach their full potential as adults. 
  - if unconditional positive regard is ==not present== in a child's early life, they will instead develop ==conditional self regard== (the belief that they are only worthy as human beings if they follow particular rules.) 
	  - instead of self actualizing, the person will become stuck and will likely experience some form of psychological abnormality.
