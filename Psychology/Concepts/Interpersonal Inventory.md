> [!important] 
## Benefits:
### To patient:
creates space to reflect on important relationships:
- discover feelings about and expectations of different relationships
- consider the nature and quality of their interactions
- identify which relationships they wish to focus on during treatment.
### To therapist:
- identify specific **relationships** that are associated with **distress**
- begin to learn about the patient’s social supports
- gather more data for case formulation and choose an interpersonal problem area.
## Goals:
> [!NOTE] beginning phase of [[Interpersonal psychotherapy|IPT]]: focus on **several** relationships instead of just 1
> revisit important relationships/issues during middle phase
- Look for 1-2 *typical interactions* in these relationships 
- pay close attention to their [[affect]]:
	- which relationship impacted by [[Depressive Disorders|depression]]
	- contributing to [[Depressive Disorders|depression]]
- learn about relationships who are supports/helpful -> recruit/ strengthen through [[Interpersonal psychotherapy|IPT]] 
What could be discovered:
- feelings
- expectations
- satisfying aspects
- reciprocity (互惠)/non-reciprocity
- difficulties
- tensions
	- ex: upsetting interaction with another person
## Questions:
- Who are the important people in your life? 
	- Can you tell me about them? 
	- What is your relationship with them like? 
	- Where would you place them on the Closeness Circles?
- _**Who raised you?**_
	- _What were they like?_
- Who ***do you turn to*** for comfort or support in times of need?
- Who ***turns to you***?
- Who do you see most days?
> [!NOTE] paraphrase with [[Reflective listening]] 
### Talking about the relationships:
- response to [[affect]] change:
	- "I can see when you speak of her that it is quite painful a transition"
	- "when you are describing wanting to xxx with these relationships, we'll be looking closer into these relationships and find different ways to bring these people close to you in [[Interpersonal psychotherapy|IPT]]"
	- "The way that you feel is likely part of your [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] and as your [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] starts to improve, that [anger/sadness/distance] with these relationships will tend to improve"
- Range of relationships: ^1b354d
	- practical supports who help with specific tasks
	- those who have shared interests or values
	- trusted confidantes with whom feelings and more personal experiences can be shared.
- activities: ^1a00b9
	- inviting a friend for coffee or to share a meal
	- setting up an online video chat
	- meeting a friend for a walk
	- visiting a museum or park together
	- connecting with a group who shares values, hobbies or interests
		- (e.g., nature, crafts, cooking, card games, creative activities, spiritual/religious beliefs, sporting activities, reading/book clubs).
## Toolkit:
### Circles of connection:
- therapist can either ask patients to describe first, or map first
- close relations are mapped, then the therapist asks the patient to describe the individuals and their respective relationships using some of the questions above.
