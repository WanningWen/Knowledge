> [!info] 
> Characterized by excessive fear and anxiety and related behavioral disturbances; Includes phobias, separation anxiety, panic disorder, generalized anxiety disorder, social anxiety disorder, agoraphobia ([[DSM V]])
> 
> Anxiety is a feeling of threat or worry caused by perceived threats in the environment which differs from everyday anxiety that it is:
> - more severe
> - persistent
> - interferes with the person's studies, activities, family and social relationships
>   
## Clinical Presentation
#### Fear vs Anxiety [[Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders|DSM]] 
- fear is an **emotional response to a real or perceived imminent threat** which leads to “…surges of autonomic arousal necessary for flight or flight, thoughts of immediate danger, and escape behaviors.” 
- Anxiety is the **anticipation of a future threat** leading to, “…**muscle tension and vigilance** in preparation for future danger and cautious or avoidant behaviors”

## Etiology
### biological causes 
#### Genetic influences. 
- genetics contribute to the presentation of anxiety symptoms
- interaction between genetics and stressful environmental account for more than genetics alone (Bienvenu, Davydow, & Kendler, 2011)
- serotonin transporter gene (5-HTTLPR):
	- Mutation of the 5-HTTLPR gene is related to a reduction in serotonin activity and an increase in anxiety-related personality traits (Munafo, Brown, & Hairiri, 2008).
#### Neurobiological structures. 
- [[amygdala]](Gorman, Kent, Sullivan, & Coplan, 2000). 
	- initiates a reaction to ready the body for a response when presented with a fearful situation
	- triggers the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis to prepare for immediate action (fight or flight)
	- sending a sensory signal to the **[[hippocampus]]** and **[[prefrontal cortex]]**, to determine if the threat is real or imagined. 
		- no threat is present --> [[amygdala]] sends a calming response to the HPA axis
		- If a threat is present--> the amygdala is activated, producing a fear response.

### psychological causes
#### Cognitive perspective: dysfunctional thought patterns
[[Rational-Emotive therapy]]
- [[maladaptive attitudes]]: interpreting events as dangerous or overreacting to potentially stressful events, which contributes to an overall heightened anxiety level
- [[negative attributional style]]/ negative appraisals
-  a biological predisposition to anxiety, likely contribute to the development of anxiety symptoms (Gallagher et al., 2013).
Potential explanation:
1. more susceptible to more frequent and intensive physiological symptoms
2. trauma-related experiences in the past
the cognitive misinterpretations to physiological arousal creates **a negative feedback loop**, leading to more physiological changes.

### sociocultural causes
- living in poverty
- experiencing significant daily stressors
- increased exposure to traumatic events
- gender and discrimination
#### women are routinely diagnosed with anxiety disorders more often than men
- influence of social pressures on women
**[[emotion-focused coping]]** vs **problem-focused coping** (McLean & Anderson, 2009) ^ab8418
- less effective in reducing distress
- increase levels of stress hormones within women that leave them susceptible to develop symptoms of anxiety
