## Symptoms
symptoms can generally be grouped into four categories: mood, behavioral, cognitive, and physical symptoms
### Mood
most, if not all individuals
- a depressed mood for most of the day 
- feelings of _[[anhedonia]]_, which is the loss of interest in previously interesting activities.
### Behavioral
decreased physical activity and reduced productivity—both at home and work
- disruption in daily functioning: unable to maintain their social interactions and employment responsibilities
### Cognitive
- a negative view of themselves and the world around them
- quick to blame themselves, and rarely take credit when they experience positive achievements
- feel worthless
	- a negative feedback loop reinforcing their overall depressed mood
- difficulty concentrating on tasks
	- individuals with depression perform worse than those without depression on tasks of memory, attention, and reasoning (Chen et al., 2013)
- thoughts of [[suicide]] and self-harm
### Physical:
- Changes in sleep patterns
	- **[[Hypersomnia]]**
	- **[[insomnia]]**
- change in weight or eating behaviors
	- a lack of appetite, often forcing themselves to eat something during the day
	- overeat, often seeking “comfort foods,” such as those high in carbohydrates
- psychomotor agitation or retardation
	-  purposeless or slowed physical movement of the body (i.e., pacing around a room, tapping toes, restlessness, etc.)

## Etiology (Cause:)
### Biology:
#### Genetics:
> [!NOTE] personalized prognosis for depression?
> depression may be tied to the 5-HTT gene on chromosome 17, as this is responsible for the activity of serotonin (Jansen et al., 2016).

Genetic predisposition: a higher rate of [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] within families than the general population
- ~30% increase in relatives diagnosed with [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] compared to 10% of the general population (Levinson & Nichols, 2014)
**twin studies** (identical twins vs fraternal twins):
- if one identical twin was diagnosed with [[depression]], 46% chance for identical twin
- rate of a [[depression]] diagnosis in fraternal twins was only 20%
- enough evidence that there is a strong genetic link in the development of [[depression]] (McGuffin et al., 1996)
Molecular level:
- depression may be tied to the 5-HTT gene on chromosome 17, as this is responsible for the activity of serotonin (Jansen et al., 2016).
#### Biochemical Deficit
- low activity levels of norepinephrine and serotonin
-  discovered accidentally in the 1950s when MAOIs were given to tuberculosis patients, and miraculously, their depressive moods were also improved
- medications used to treat high blood pressure by causing a reduction in norepinephrine also caused depression in their patients (Ayd, 1956).
- hormones, particularly **cortisol**, a hormone released as a stress response, in the development of depression (Owens et al., 2014)
- **melatonin**, a hormone released when it is dark outside to assist with the transition to sleep, may also be related to depressive symptoms, particularly during the winter months
#### Brain Anatomy:
- drastic changes in blood flow throughout the [[prefrontal cortex]] have been linked with [[Depressive Disorders]]
- a smaller [[hippocampus]]-> fewer [[neurons]] linked to [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] symptoms
- heightened activity and blood flow in the [[amygdala]] consistently found in individuals with depressive symptoms
### Cognitive:
[[Cognitive Behavioral Model]],  focuses on the negative thoughts and perceptions of an individual
#### [[Learned helplessness]]
#### [[positive attributional style]] vs [[negative attributional style]]
- individuals with a [[negative attributional style]] are more likely to experience depression
- if something bad were to happen to them, they would conclude that it is _their_ fault (internal), bad things _always_ happen to them (stable), and bad things happen _all_ day to them.
#### Negative thinking
Aaron Beck coined these terms used in [[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy]]:
- [[maladaptive attitudes]]
- [[cognitive triad]]
- errors in thinking/ [[Cognitive Distortions]]
- [[automatic thoughts]]
- rumination:
	- rumination of negative thoughts is positively related to an increase in depression symptoms (Hankin, 2009).
### Behavioral:
depression as a result of ==a change in the number of rewards and punishments one receives== throughout their life (work, intimate relationships, family, or even the environment)
Peter Lewinsohn: depression occurred in most people due to **reduced positive rewards** in their life.
- Because they were not positively rewarded, their constructive behaviors occurred more infrequently until they stop engaging in the behavior completely (Lewinsohn et al., 1990; 1984).
-  An example of this is a student who keeps receiving bad grades on their exam despite studying for hours. Over time, the individual will reduce the amount of time they are studying, thus continuing to earn poor grades.
### Socialcultural
the role of family and one’s social environment
####  _family-social perspective_
depression is related to the ==unavailability of social support==
- ==separated and divorced== individuals are three times more likely to experience depressive symptoms than those that are married or even widowed (Schultz, 2007).
- ==Depressive symptoms have been positively related to increased interpersonal conflicts==, reduced communication, and intimacy issues, all of which are often reported in causal factors leading to a divorce (Najman et al., 2014).
- stress and marital discord leading to increased rates of depression in one or both spouses (Nezlek et al., 2000).
-  women who had three or more young children, and also lacked a close confidante and outside employment, were more likely than other mothers to become depressed (Brown, 2002).
#### _multi-cultural perspective._
one’s cultural background may influence _what_ symptoms of depression are presented
- individuals from ==non-Western countries== (China and other Asian countries) often focus on the ==physical symptoms of depression==—tiredness, weakness, sleep issues—and less of an emphasis on the cognitive symptoms.
difference in the rate of recurrence of depression in Hispanic and African Americans (Gonzalez et al., 2010)
- propose a lack of treatment opportunities as a possible explanation
- approximately 54% of depressed white Americans seek out treatment, compared to the 34% and 40% Hispanic and African Americans
#### Gender differences
[[Gender causes of depression]]

> [!question] Given the article, what are your thoughts about what comprehensive and appropriate treatment should look like?
> - specialized personal treatment towards depression while taking into account various social factors

##### _artifact theory_
difference between genders is due to clinician or diagnostic systems being more sensitive to diagnosing women with depression than men
##### _hormone theory_
variations in hormone levels trigger depression in women more than men (Graziottin & Serafini, 2009).
- research fails to support this theory as the reason for higher rates of depression in women (Whiffen & Demidenko, 2006).
##### _life stress theory_
women are more likely to experience chronic stressors than men, thus accounting for their higher rate of depression (Astbury, 2010)
-  increased risk for poverty, lower employment opportunities, discrimination, and poorer quality of housing than men, all of which are strong predictors of depressive symptoms (Garcia-Toro et al., 2013).
##### _gender roles theory_
For example
- men -> develop personal autonomy, personal fulfillment, achievement-oriented goals; 
- women -> empathize and care for others -> value the opinion of others more highly than their male counterparts do
##### _rumination theory_
women are more likely than men to ruminate on their depressive symptoms -> vulnerable to develop clinical depression (Nolen-Hoeksema, 2012)
