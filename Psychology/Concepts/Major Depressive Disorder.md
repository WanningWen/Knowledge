> [!info] To meet criteria for a diagnosis in [[DSM V|DSM-5-TR]]:
> - at least _five_ symptoms across the four categories (Mood, Behavioral, Cognitive, Physical) in [[Depressive Disorders]] 
> - and at least one of the symptoms:
> 	1) a depressed mood most of the day, almost every day
> 	2) loss of interest or pleasure in all, or most, activities, most of the day, almost every day.
> - symptoms must be present for _at least two weeks_ and cause clinically significant distress or impairment in important areas of functioning such as social and occupational

^57f627
> [!NOTE] Lay people explanation:
> Sad or low mood for at least 2 weeks
## Prevalence/ Epidemiology
- 6.9% (16 million) of American adults live with major depression. (Source: Major Depression Among Adults.(n.d.).  #Nonprofit/Org/NAMI 
- [[DSM-5-TR]] (APA, 2022): ^66b493
	- the 12-month prevalence rate is approximately 7% within the United States
	- ==18- 29 year-old== age bracket report the highest rates of MDD than any other age group
	- ==Women== experience about twofold higher rates than men of MDD, especially between **menarche** and **menopause** (APA, 2022)
		- lifetime prevalence in women is 21.3% vs 12.7% in men (Nolen-Hoeksema, 2001)

## Diagnosis:
The [[DSM V|DSM-5-TR]] cautions responses to a significant loss can lead to many of the symptoms but this may be the normal response instead of clinical depression
- clinical judgment should be utilized in making any diagnosis and be based on the individual’s ==personal history== and ==cultural norms== related to how members should express distress in the context of loss.
