> [!important] "nomenclature" (set of terms) to classify different types of self-directed violence
>  developed by the Rocky Mountain MIRECC with the Center for Disease Control and Prevention
>  Purpose: make the language more precise and consistent
>  22 different terms -- spectrum of self-directed violence related thoughts and behaviors 
## Introduction
Self-directed violence encompasses a ==wide range of phenomena== that include ==both thoughts and behaviors==, and this may or may not reflect suicidal intent. 
==Variations with respect to injury and interruption== are also important differentiating factors. 
- For example, some forms of self-directed violence result in injury, some do not, while others are fatal. 
Historically, there has been a ==lack of agreed upon terms or "nomenclature"== to describe the range of thoughts and behaviors that fall under the larger umbrella of self-directed violence. 
- Using different terms to describe suicidal and non-suicidal thoughts and behavior ==results in confusion and misunderstanding==.
### Terms carry a certain level of judgement
- inaccurate assumptions about the individual's experience
> [!example] "cry for help" "gesture" "suicidal episode"
- inconsistent assessment of risk --> improper treatment
- impede communication between and amongst clinicians, patients, patients' families
- inconsistent reporting -- public health and research implications
### Correct terminology should:
- covers the whole spectrum of suicidal and nonsuicidal thoughts and behaviors

## Key Concepts:
### Differentiate thoughts vs. Behaviors
> [!NOTE] Behavior: requires that the person makes acts/ preparations towards engaging in SDV

"Behaviors trump thought": we don't have to establish the presence of thought if behavior is there 
> [!question] thought --> whether there is intent to act on those thoughts
#### Behavior:
- was the injury fatal
- was the behavior interrupted by self/other?
> [!NOTE] [[Interruption By Self or Other]]
> act by a person, not by the rope breaking

##### questions to determine suicidal intent

> [!question] "When you downloaded that information, did you have any wish to die"

> [!question] "Let's take a step back for a moment and think back to right before you (stepped off the stool), was it your intent that you wanted to die?"

> [!question] "Did you mean to kill yourself by doing that or was there any other types of motivation" 

> [!question] "You said that during times when you get mad, you think of ways to hurt yourself. Could you clarify a little more. Do you ever think about ways to kill yourself? "

Key Terms (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention)

| [[Self-Directed Violence]]         | Behavior that is self-directed and deliberately results in injury or the potential for injury to oneself.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| [[Suicidal Intent]]:               | There is past or present evidence (implicit or explicit) that an individual wishes to die, means to kill him/herself, and understands the probable consequences of his, her actions or potential actions. intent can be determined retrospectively and in the absence of suicidal behavior.                                                                                                                                                |
| [[Preparatory Behavior]]:          | Acts or preparation towards engaging in [[Self-Directed Violence]], but before potential for injury has begun. This can include anything beyond a verbalization or thought, such as assembling a method (e.g., buying a gun, collecting pills) or preparing for one's death by suicide (e.g., writing a suicide note, giving things away).                                                                                                 |
| [[Physical Injury ]](paraphrased): | A bodily lesion resulting from acute overexposure to energy (this can be mechanical , thermal, electrical, chemical, or radiant) interacting with the body in amounts or rates that exceed the threshold of physiological tolerance (e.g., bocily harm due to suffocation, poisoning or overdoses, lacerations, gunshot vicunds, etc.).  Refer to the Classification System for the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention definition. |
| [[Interruption By Self or Other]]: | A person takes steps to injure self but is stopped by self/another person prior to fatal injury.<br><br>The interruption may occur at any point.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Suicide Attempt:                   | A non-fatal self-inflicted potentially injurious behavior with any intent to die as a result of the behavior.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
