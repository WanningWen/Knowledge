## Reasons for documentation:
- allow other team members (doctors, counselors, nurses) to better understand
	- medication side effects
		- ex: sudden loss of bladder control, sexual side effects, etc.
- agencies cannot get paid for work which is undocumented
	- ==The funding source for the agency with which you work will determine the type of documentation required.==
	- federal funds (Medicare and/or federally run agencies)
	- state funds (from [[Medi-Cal]] or other state funded agency)
	- county funds (from county contracts)
	- insurance companies.
	- [[Senate Bill 614]] is passed, allowing Medi-Cal to specifically reimburse PSSs at a specific rate.
- ![[continuity of care]]
- allows the team to be cohesive. If one team member finds out that a client is going to have surgery, putting that information in the case notes allows all team members to know about the surgery.
- building [[rapport]] on small details

## Documenting practices
- Type of Service Provided
- Recovery Language
	- the possibility that a person or their family can request a copy of your agency’s case notes at any time. Should the person have the opportunity to read their own notes, you want them to recognize the hope you have for them.
- Neutrality
	- Neutrality means that your documentation must demonstrate what happened, not how you felt about what happened.
	- if you went to meet with a family member and that family member screamed obscenities at you, you would simply document it as, “Client screamed obscenities” not “Client screamed obscenities and made me angry.”
- Monitoring outcome achievements
	- Identifying these achievements will allow you to ==help the family member also learn how to identify the achievements==.
		- documented prior achievements/successes help family members see hope in the recovery journey
	- [[Certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist|MPSSS]] are "carriers of hope"
- Communication: [[Reflective listening|Active Listening]]
- Use descriptive language and avoid acronyms or jargon
- Write in person-centered, person-first, non-stigmatizing language that is easy to understand
- Focus on the mental health and/or substance use disorder interventions provided during the encounter
- Ensure documentation is accurate, precise, and timely
- Clearly document the behavioral health intervention
- Identify next steps for the person in care, their support system, and/or the certified Peer Support Specialist
- Adhere to confidentiality regulations
- Recognize that progress notes serve as a legal record describing treatment provided, including for reimbursement purposes
- Provide services only within one’s scope of practice
- Follow program-specific policies and procedures, as processes may differ
