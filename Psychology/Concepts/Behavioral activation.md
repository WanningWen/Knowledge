> [!important]  increase the frequency of rewarding behaviors --> experience greater contact with sources of reward in their lives
> goal of treatment (similar to [[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy|CBT]]) is to alleviate [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] and prevent future relapse by changing an individual’s behavior
### History:
Founded by Ferster (1973), as well as Lewinsohn and colleagues (Lewinsohn, 1974; Lewinsohn, Biglan, & Zeiss, 1976)
## Methodology:
1. developing a list of pleasurable activities that they can engage in outside of treatment 
		(i.e., going for a walk, going shopping, having dinner with a friend). 
2.  identifying their negative behaviors and monitoring them so that they do not impact the outcome of their pleasurable activities. 
		—crying, sleeping in, avoiding friends—
3. effective social skills.
the individual will receive more positive reward and reinforcement from others and their environment, thus improving their overall mood.
