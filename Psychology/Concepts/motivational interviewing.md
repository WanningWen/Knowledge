> [!important] A directive, person-centered counseling style for eliciting behavior change by helping people to explore & resolve ambivalence. *(Miller & Rollnick, 2002)*
# Definition
a **particular tool for a specific scenario**: when a person may need to make a [[Behavioral change]] or lifestyle change and is reluctant/ ambivalent about doing so
- designed to ==strengthen personal motivation== for and commitment to a specific goal by eliciting and exploring the person's own reasons for change within an atmosphere of acceptance and compassion
#Career/PeerSupportSpecialist/Session #course/Coursera [[Coursera - Abnormal Psychology]]
- way to structure collaborative, goal-oriented style of communication --> particular attention to language of change ([[change talk]])
#### What Motivational Interviewing IS NOT
- a [[Transtheoretical model of change]]
-  a school of psycho-therapy
- comprehensive treatment
# Concept:
- until a person is motivated to change, there is not much we can do 
## What is Motivation:
=="motivation can be understood not as something that one *has*, but something that one *does*"==
- recognizing a problem
- searching for a way to change
- then beginning and sticking with that change strategy
#### key element that comes before change: ==**ambivalence**==:
> [!NOTE] **ambivalence**: 
> an individual's own statements in favor of making a change

> [!NOTE] **[[sustain talk]]:** 
> an individual's own statements in favor of staying the same

unfavorable scenario: battling between self defense mechanisms to stay the same and the person who's trying to convince you to make a change

## Building Blocks: Engaging, Focusing, Evoking, Planning
### 1. Engaging 
Process by which both members of a diet establish a connection/working relationship.
"building [[Rapport]]"
> fundamental to any type of [[diabetic interactions]] w psychology
##### 2. Focusing 
Developing/ moving forward with a conversation about change/the decision to be made: what is hoped to be accomplished by the interaction/treatment
##### 3. Evoking: eliciting client's own personal motivation to change
therapist help client get in touch with their own values and reasons for change 
- [[critical listening skills]] 
- [[decisional balance]] exercises
==sustaining and conversationally reinforcing **[[change talk]]**==

**change talk**: client statements about their desire, ability, reasons, need to change
> more change talk --> more motivation to change --> more actual behavior
##### 4. Planning
> both **commitment to change** once that decision is reached & **practical planning** of figuring out exactly what that change should be + how to go about doing it

### What NOT to do:
#### MI tries to correct/protect against [[righting reflex]] 
- righting reflex causes the patient/client to stand up harder for their position
- MI seeks to help counselor/listener/friend "drop the rope" instead of getting into a tug of war
	- patient explore their position/value --> come to a decision and make an action plan that makes sense for them
- avoid accusations & arguments
	- accusatory: "are you smoking" --> puts patient on the defensive & minimizes likelihood of productive discussion
- [[Reflective listening|active listening]] instead of lecturing
	- lecture and scold parent ignoring multiple remarks of low confidence in ability to successfully quit smoking
	- patient eventually felt completely defeated, caved in and announced she would quit smoking despite **no intention to quit smoking at this time but to bring an end to the uncomfortable confrontation**

***INSTEAD OF:***
- take advantage of opportunities to discuss behavior change
- work collaboratively with the patient
