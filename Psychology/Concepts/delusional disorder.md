## Clinical Presentation
- presence of at least one [[delusion]] that lasts for at least one month in duration.
	- but no other [[Schizophrenia]] symptoms (positive, negative, disorganized)
- daily functioning is not overly impacted due to the delusions
- brief depressive or manic episodes compared to the duration of the delusions
![[delusion]]
### ==Modifier==: 5 Subtypes of [[delusional disorder]] by [[DSM V|DSM-5-TR]]
#### Erotomanic delusion 
a delusion of _another person_ being in love with them. 
- "victim" is of higher status, such as a celebrity. 
#### Grandiose delusion 
the conviction of having great talent or insight. 
- patients will report they have made an important discovery that benefits the general public. 
- religious affiliation, as people believe they are prophets or even God. 
#### Jealous delusion
- thinks one’s spouse or partner is/has been unfaithful. 
- much more extensive and generally based on incorrect inferences that lack evidence. 
#### Persecutory delusion 
- thinks they are conspired against, spied on, followed, poisoned or drugged, maliciously maligned, harassed, or obstructed in pursuit of their long-term goals (APA, 2022). 
- most at risk of becoming aggressive or hostile, likely due to the persecutory nature of their distorted beliefs. 
#### somatic delusion 
- delusions regarding bodily functions or sensations. 
	- the most common beliefs are that the individual emits a foul odor despite attempts to rectify the smell; there is an infestation of insects on the skin; or that they have an internal parasite (APA, 2022).
#### mixed type/ unspecific type [[specifier]]
#### bizarre
- the content of the  delusions are deemed **bizarre** or implausible, not understandable, and not derived from ordinary life experience.
