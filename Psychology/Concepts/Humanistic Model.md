> [!important] believe that all people are essentially good and whole, and that a primary goal of human experience is to fully self actualize, that is to achieve one's fullest potential as a human being in all areas of life. 
> all people need is the freedom to grow and they will achieve self actualization.

 Development and application have addressed the drawbacks of the biological model, the psychodynamic model and the cognitive behavioral model.
  
- believe that both the biological and cognitive behavioral models are too simplistic and narrow to fully explain the rich complexity of human experience
- psychodynamic model does a better job trying to engage with the whole person, not just their neurotransmitter levels or their cognitive distortions. The psychodynamic theorists are nonetheless too negative, they focus only on the difficult part of of human existence.

However, when barriers are imposed on their growth from the external world, they are not able to self actualize, and instead their growth is blocked and results in the experience of psychological abnormality.

## Strengths and Weaknesses:
 The humanistic model has many strengths, it fills in a large gap left by the biological, psychodynamic, and cognitive behavioral models. It presents a far more growth oriented, and optimistic view of human experience. It's focused on self actualization, self acceptance, personal values, goals and meaning are all very important areas of struggle for people who have psychological disorders. Person centered therapy has provided for a great deal of research into the therapeutic relationship between the therapist and the client, which prior to its development had not been systematically studied. 

These are all strengths of the model, one major drawback, however, is that it is difficult to study the concepts that are so important to this model. As a result, there is very little research supporting the validity of the humanistic model of psychological abnormality, which is a clear weakness when comparing it to the biological and cognitive behavioral models. Both of which boast a large research base
