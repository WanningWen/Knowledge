> [!important]  _persistent_ over-accumulation of possessions (APA, 2022)
## Clinical Presentation
commonly items:
- newspapers, magazines, clothes, bags, books, mail, and paperwork (APA, 2022)
impairment in social and occupational functioning; low quality of life:
- items clutter living space, unable to use furniture: place the individual at risk for figure, falling, poor sanitation, and other health risks
- hold onto these non-sentimental items are fear of losing valuable information and fear of being wasteful
- significant distress with cleaning out items 
- strained relationship with family
- conflict with neighbor/local authorities
## Epidemiology/ Prevalence
- 1.5% to 6.0% of the population
- females than males
- over the age of 65 years are 3x more likely to be diagnosed  than younger adults
## [[comorbidity]]
- 75% have a comorbid mood or anxiety disorder 
	- major depressive disorder, social anxiety disorder, and generalized anxiety disorder are the most common
- nearly 20% also meet the criteria for OCD (APA, 2022).
## Etiology

# Treatment
no intrusive thoughts/ urge for rituals --> no exposure and response prevention, and more toward a traditional cognitive-behavioral approach
### [[cognitive restructuring]] and [[motivational interviewing]]
address the complex-decision making that is involved in maintaining unnecessary possessions
- discussing motives for keeping items, as well as fears that may be associated with discarding items
- ultimately determine the item’s actual worth (Williams & Viscusi, 2016)
- many individuals in treatment for hoarding disorder prematurely end treatment, thus never reaching remission of symptoms (Mancebo, Eisen, Sibrava, Dyck, & Rasmussen, 2011).
