> [!important] an [[Exposure treatments]] technique that utilizes relaxation strategies to help calm the individual as they are presented with the fearful object
- both fear and relaxation cannot exist at the same time; therefore, the individual learns how to replace their fearful reaction with a calm, relaxing reaction
1. the patient, with assistance from the clinician, will identify a _fear hierarchy_, or a list of feared objects/situations ordered from least fearful to most fearful
2. After teaching several different types of relaxation techniques, the clinician will present items from the fear hierarchy, starting from the least fearful object/subject, while the patient practices using the learned relaxation techniques
	feared object/situation can be in person—**in vivo exposure**—or it can be imagined—**imaginal exposure**
3. Once the patient can effectively employ relaxation techniques to reduce their [[Anxiety Disorders|anxiety]] to a manageable level, the clinician will slowly move up the fear hierarchy until the individual does not experience excessive fear of all objects on the list
