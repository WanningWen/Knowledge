> [!important]  a mental health condition and one of the main sources of maternal suffering and poor health,
> Symptoms of the condition usually appear within the first 4 to 6 weeks after giving birth but may also develop up to 1 year after delivery. 
> 
> The most commonly occurring symptoms include:
> - Anxiety
> - Irritability
> - Feelings of being overwhelmed
> - Lack of interest in the infant
> - Mood fluctuations
> - Obsessive preoccupation with the infant’s health and feeding
> - Periods of weeping
> - Possible thoughts of suicide
> - Worries of causing harm to the infant

^6dca84

## Prevalence
- a prevalence of approximately 10% to 15% worldwide.
- Pregnancy-related mental health deaths account for 20% of all pregnancy-related mortalities in the United States and are a primary cause of preventable, pregnancy-related deaths. 
- From 2008 to 2017, more than 60% of pregnancy-related mental health mortalities occurred in the time frame of 43 to 365 days after delivery.
- Over 4 million women give birth annually in the United States, and there is evidence suggesting that approximately 400,000 to 800,000 women may experience PPD.6 
- Approximately 85% of women will experience some kind of emotional disturbance after delivery, and PPD may occur in up to 30% of cases, with greater prevalence in middle- and low-income countries.
## Diagnosis:
[[DSM V]]: a PPD diagnosis would be specified as [[Major Depressive Disorder|major depressive disorder]] with the perinatal qualifier: onset during late pregnancy or within 4 weeks postpartum. However
- defined as a moderate to severe depressive episode starting 4 weeks after delivery.
-  should not be **confused with the “baby blues,”** which can occur within the first 10 days after delivery and is characterized by mild mood disturbances.
- PPD and postpartum anxiety are 2 distinct conditions and should be treated as such
> [!danger] distinguished from postpartum psychosis
> - rare and occurs in 1 to 2 women per 1000
> - hallucinations, bizarre behavior, confusion, or disorganized thoughts, accompanied by a depressed or elevated mood

## Impact:
### On infants:
- Infant temperament
- Maternal/infant interactions
- Duration of breastfeeding
- Engagement in activities that promote infant safety and maintain health
## Risk Factors:
Genetic and social factors may also play a role
- A previous history of depression or mental health condition
- Depressive symptoms during pregnancy
- Low levels of spousal and/or social support
- Obstetrical and/or neonatal complications
- Stressful life events
- Gestational diabetes
- History of abuse
- Negative birth experience
Weak associations:
- Low socioeconomic status
- Body image dissatisfaction
- Pre-pregnancy obesity
- Cesarean-section birth
- Multiple births
### Data:
3 in 5 women with PPD symptoms at 9 and 10 months post-delivery, more than half did not report these symptoms at 2 to 6 months postpartum. 

Those with prior depressive symptoms
- almost 75% of participants who had PPD symptoms both at 2 to 6 months and 9 to 10 months had a prior bout of depression either before or during pregnancy.
- women who have untreated depression during pregnancy are more than 7 times more likely to have PPD, compared with women who have no antenatal depression symptoms.
