> [!important] anxiety or fear relates to social situations, particularly those in which an individual can be evaluated by others
>  the individual is worried that they will be judged negatively (stupid, anxious, crazy, boring, or unlikeable)
>  These fears severely limit an individual’s behavior in social settings and have occurred for six months or more.

^8cb3a8
## Comorbidity:
- most common:
	- other anxiety-related disorders
	- [[Major Depressive Disorder|major depressive disorder]]: chronic social isolation
	- substance-related disorders: the efforts of self-medicating to deal with social fears
-  body dysmorphic disorder and avoidant personality disorder
## Etiology (causes)
[[Cognitive Behavioral Model|Cognitive Behavioral Theory]]:  individuals tend to hold unattainable or extremely high social beliefs and expectations
- [[maladaptive attitudes|maladaptive assumptions]]: "they will behave incompetently in social situations and that their behaviors will lead to terrible consequences"  (Moscovitch et al., 2013)
- Individuals with social anxiety disorder will also evaluate the social event after it has taken place, often obsessively reviewing the details. This overestimation of social performance negatively reinforces future avoidance of social situations.
## Treatment:
### [[Exposure treatments]] 
1. role-play: practice social interactions in a safe, controlled environment (Rodebaugh, Holaway, & Heimberg, 2004).
2. venture outside of the treatment room + engage in social situations with random strangers at various locations such as fast-food restaurants, local stores, libraries, etc. 
3. continue exposures outside of treatment
### [[Social skills training]]
focuses on the patient’s skill deficits or inadequate social interactions: a combination of skills such as [[Psychological Modeling]], corrective feedback, and positive reinforcement to provide feedback and encouragement to the patient regarding their behavioral interactions (Rodebaugh, Holaway, & Heimberg, 2004). 
Goal: the patient can engage in positive social behaviors outside of the treatment room and improve their overall social interactions while reducing ongoing social anxiety.
### [[cognitive restructuring]]/[[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy|CBT]]
- an additive component in treatment to provide substantial symptom reduction. 
- help the patient establish new, positive thoughts to replace these negative thoughts. 
- ==implementing cognitive restructuring techniques before, during, and after [[Exposure treatments]] ==sessions enhances the overall effects of treatment (Heimberg & Becker, 2002).
