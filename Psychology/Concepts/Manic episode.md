## Diagnosis:
> [!NOTE]  At least three of the symptoms described below must be present and represent a noticeable change in the individual’s typical behavior.
> (four if the mood is only irritable) 
### Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity (Criterion B1)
For example:
- individuals may believe they are friends with a celebrity, do not need to abide by laws, or even perceive themselves as God. 
- They also engage in multiple overlapping new projects (Criteria B6 and 7), often initiated with no prior knowledge about the topic, and engaged in at unusual hours of the day.
### require a decreased need for sleep (Criterion B2)
sleeping as little as a few hours a night yet still feeling rested
### disregarding the reciprocal nature of communication (Criterion B3)
more talkative than usual. It can be difficult to follow their conversation due to the quick pace of their talking, as well as tangential storytelling
- can be difficult to interrupt in conversation
- more irritable than expansive
- speech can become hostile and they engage in tirades, particularly if they are interrupted or not allowed to engage in an activity they are seeking out (APA, 2022).
