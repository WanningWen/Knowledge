> [!important] [[Antidepressant]] that block the absorption or reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine, thus increasing their availability for postsynaptic neurons
> increasingly replaced by SSRIs due to their reduced side effects
### History
Originally developed to treat [[schizophrenia]],  adapted to treat [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] after failing to manage symptoms of schizophrenia (Kuhn, 1958)

## Effect:
- increasingly replaced by SSRIs due to their reduced side effects
- more effective in treating depressive symptoms in individuals who have not been able to achieve symptom reduction via other pharmacological approaches
## Side-effects:
mild:
- dry mouth
- blurry vision
- constipation
Severe:
- sexual dysfunction
- tachycardia 
- cognitive and/or memory impairment
SHOULD NOT be used in cardiac patients as they may exacerbate cardiac arrhythmias (Roose & Spatz, 1999).
