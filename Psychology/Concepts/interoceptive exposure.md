> [!important] Type of [[Exposure treatments]] 
> inducing panic-specific symptoms to the individual repeatedly for a prolonged period, so that maladaptive thoughts about the sensations can be disconfirmed and conditional anxiety responses are extinguished (Craske & Barlow, 2014)
## Theory:
- aim: correct inaccurate estimate of cognitive symptoms (fear/panic)
- patient must endure the physiological sensations for at least 30 seconds to 1 minute
	- this time is enough to use cognitive strategies to understand how the patient feels (Craske & Barlow, 2014)
- We want them to use these cognitive strategies as a habit --> long-term remission of panic symptoms (Foa & McNally, 1996).
## Example:
- spinning a patient repeatedly in a chair to induce dizziness
- breathing in a paper bag to cause hyperventilation
