## Studies
### Mark Seligman's Dog
despite the opportunity to escape the shock, the dogs flurried for a bit, and then ultimately laid down and whimpered while being shocked Seligman concluded that the ==animals learned that they were unable to avoid the shock the day prior==, and therefore, learned that they were helpless in preventing the shocks. When they were placed in a similar environment but had the opportunity to escape the shock, their ==learned helplessness carried over, and they continued to believe they were unable to escape the shock==.

Martin Seligman restrained dogs in an apparatus and routinely shocked them regardless of their behavior. The following day, the dogs were placed in a similar apparatus; however, this time they were not restrained and there was a small barrier placed between the “shock” floor and the “safe” floor.
