> [!important] Model of Treatment in mental health
## Main Idea: Recovering **One’s Life**, rather than Recovering **from an Illness**
Diagnosis ≠ Prognosis
- Person living with the diagnosis is the expert
- A Person can learn ==coping skills or “Tools” to deal with symptoms== 
- A Person can become ==less affected by symptoms ==as they become ==more adept at using their coping “Tools”== 
- A Person can begin the recovery journey ==regardless of how long they’ve had symptoms== 
- There is ==no deadline== on potential

| Non-Recovery Environment                                     | Recovery Environment                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Low expectations                                             | Hopeful with high expectations                                     |
| Stability / maintenance is the goal                          | Recovery is the goal                                               |
| There is no clearly defined exit                             | Clear exits; graduates return and share                            |
| Little or no access to information                           | Easy access to information                                         |
| Compliance is valued                                         | Self-determination, critical thinking, and independence are valued |
| Coercion is used to achieve compliance                       | People become experts in their own care                            |
| People are protected from their own trial-and-error learning | People take risks and have the “right to fail”                     |
| One-size-fits-all treatment approach                         | Wide range of programs and non-program options                     |
| People live in residential treatment centers                 | Opportunities for community integration with choice                |
| People are judged by their level of motivation               | Restoring hope creates new choices                                 |
| Medication is the primary tool                               | Medication is one of several tools                                 |
| Emphasis is on medical treatment                             | Peer support and self-help are valued                              |

## Keys to Recovery:
### Choice & Accountability
How can having the **opportunity to make choices about the treatment** one receives impact one’s **willingness to take charge** of & own their treatment?
#### Creating choice:
- People are only willing to make choices when they believe that their choice will make a difference. 
- Making choices for someone else robs them of taking ownership of that choice & being held accountable for the consequences

How can holding someone accountable for their actions support Peers & Family Members
### Empowerment
- Break through Learned Helplessness by helping develop [[Explanatory styles]]
- Create a [[Wellness Recovery Action Plan]] (WRAP)
- Encourage the Usage of Community Resources
- Help to Build a "Recovery Toolbox"
- Remind Peers that They are the Expert in Their Lives
- Remind Family Members that They are the Expert in Their Lives
### Create a Recovery Environment
Expressed Emotion
- Criticism
- Hostility
- Emotional over involvement

A person responds to the language used around them
#### Becoming Fluent in [[Recovery Language]]
- Pay attention to who people really are & foster empowerment based on that
- When referring to people you know & love, **show respect by using their names** 
- Ask yourself, “What is the underlying reason for this behavior?” 
- Approach all interaction based on person’s strengths, this lifts everyone up
When **talking about someone’s diagnosis, ask yourself** four things: 
1. Do I have their **permission** to talk to others about their diagnosis? 
2. Am I **billing their insurance** through this conversation? 
3. Am I **clinically diagnosing** them through this conversation? 
4. Am I writing a **prescription** for them through this conversation?
