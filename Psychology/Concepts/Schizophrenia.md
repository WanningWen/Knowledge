> [!important] 
> Disorders characterized by one or more of the following: delusions, hallucinations, disorganized thinking and speech, disorganized motor behavior, and negative symptoms ([[DSM V]])
## Clinical Presentation
- varies a lot between individuals: [[heterogeneous clinical syndrome]] (APA, 2022).
at least two of the following during a one month period: 
#symptoms
- [[delusion]]
- [[hallucinations]]
- disorganized speech
- disorganized/abnormal behavior
- [[negative symptoms]] 
Causes:
- significant impairment in an individual’s ability to engage in normal daily functioning such as work, school, relationships with others, or self-care
- continuous signs of the disturbance **persist for at least 6 months**
 “subthreshold” forms of psychotic symptoms:
- **[[prodromal symptoms]]** precede the active phase of the disorder
- **[[residual symptoms]]** that follow it.
Mood disorder symptoms:
- psychotic features will exist beyond the remission of depressive symptoms
### History:
- formally identified and ​described in 1899 by ​a German psychiatrist named Emil Kraepelin (as dementia praecox)
- ​In 1908, Kraeplin's colleague ​Eugen Bleuler first used the term schizophrenia (greek for split and mind) 
	- root of the disorder to be: inability to associate ​one thought to another thought in ​a way we would consider logical and understandable
## Epidemiology/ Prevalence
- 1 in 100 (2.4 million) American adults live with schizophrenia (Schizophrenia. (n.d.). Retrieved January 16, 2015,)
- approximately 0.3%-0.7% of the general population (APA, 2022)
- typically occurs between late teens and mid-30s
### gender:
- equal risk for both genders
- men: more [[negative symptoms]]
- women: more affect-laden symptoms (lack of emotions, etc.)
### prognosis
##### onset:
 Earlier onset of the disorder is generally predictive of a worse overall prognosis.
