> [!important] greater intensity and for longer periods of worry than the average person (APA, 2022)
> underlying excessive anxiety and worry related to a wide range of events or activities and lasting for more days than not for at least six months

^703e5a
## Diagnosis:
three or more of the following somatic symptoms:
- restlessness, fatigue, difficultly concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, and problems sleeping (APA, 2022; Gelenberg, 2000).
## Epidemiology/Prevalence:
- The 12-month prevalence is estimated to be 2.9% of the adult general population of the United States  ^7763fa
	- the mean 12-month prevalence around the world is 1.3% (with a range of 0.2% to 4.3%). 
- more frequently:
	- women and adolescent girls
	- European descent
	- high-income countries (APA, 2022).
## [[comorbidity]]
- Common ^9f4531
	- other anxiety-related disorders
	- unipolar [[Depressive Disorders]]
	- higher levels of suicidal ideation
		- most frequent anxiety disorder diagnosed in suicides: from behavior and psychological autopsy studies (APA, 2022).
Less Common:
- substance use, neurodevelopmental, neurocognitive, psychotic, and conduct disorders
# Treatment
## Psychopharmacology:
### [[Benzodiazepines]]
### [[Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors]] and [[serotonin-norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors]] (SNRIs)
a 30-50% positive response rate to these psychopharmacological interventions (Reinhold & Rickels, 2015)
- ==DO NOT provide any benefit once they are stopped==
### [[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy]]
- 60 percent of individuals report a significant reduction/elimination in anxious thoughts one-year post treatment (Hanrahan, Field, Jones, & Davy, 2013)
