> [!important]  understanding the strengths and abilities, needs and challenges, of each individual and understanding that individual’s personal recovery vision
> not only on mental health recovery but overall health as well
traditional care has tended to ==focus more on symptoms and “deficits”== and not on the whole person and the quality of their life. 

includes ==understanding what wellness, self-responsibility, empowerment, and having a meaningful role as part of the community== means for each individual. Each person has his or her own values, priorities, and perspectives that should shape the kinds of help he or she receives—acknowledging and respecting those is also part of being person-centered. In this way, person-centered care is also culturally competent care. 
## person-centered planning? 
#### [[treatment plan]]/a recovery plan/ individual plan.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> the written record of the agreements and decisions between the person and the other members of the treatment team, along with other important people in the individual’s life.  

Traditionally:
planning meetings were organized by the provider, and did not always include the service user. 

In a person-centered process:
the meeting should be held at a ==mutually agreed upon time and place==, and should be ==directed==, as much as possible,==by the person—including decisions about who should attend==. 
### person-centered process
1. recognition of the individual’s ==strengths and personal assets ==and should help the person to identify his or her ==own unique recovery goal==. 
2. identify the ==barriers to that recovery vision== :
	- mental health problems
	- general health problems
	- substance misuse
	- other needs or concerns. 
3. identify just ==a few small, but meaningful, short-term changes== that the individual and the other members of the treatment team can focus on and that ==may help to reduce the barriers or challenges== to moving forward. 
4. reach agreement on the kinds of services, activities, supports, and resources the individual needs to achieve the short term changes. 
### What’s a goal, and what’s an objective? 

> [!IMPORTANT] Goal: 
> a “big picture” change or accomplishment in the individual’s life
> 
> should reflect or include those things that are most important to the individual. 

They **don’t need to be very specific** and ideally should be stated in the **person’s own words**. 

> [!IMPORTANT] Objectives 
> the short-term intermediate goals that help people move toward realizing their larger long-term goal. 

Objectives in a plan should ==describe the specific changes in skills and abilities that the individual will achieve as a result of getting help== and working on their recovery and ==how long these changes will take==. 

They can also be thought of as the ==recovery markers== along the road to the person’s destination. 

Ideally, everyone receiving mental health or substance abuse services ==should know clearly what the goal of their plan is==, as well as ==the specific and current objective(s)== that are the focus of services. 
### What are interventions/services/supports? 
These can include diverse professional services
- clinical, rehabilitative, and medical care
- Interventions should also include 
	- peer support 
	- “natural” supports
		- such as help from family members, community organizations, wellness centers, friends, and faith-based connections
- these supports help the individual to connect with, and be part of, the life of the community. 

### Why is having a person-centered plan important? 
Most ==service providers are required by law or regulation to develop a treatment plan for each individual as part of being paid for the services they provide==. 

The plan can be thought of as ==a contract or agreement between the consumer and the provider ==to accomplish specific long- and short-term goals. 
- the likelihood of attaining goals and objectives is increased many times over
 
Ideally the plan is ==reviewed== by the individual and the treatment team==at least every 3 months== to determine whether progress is being made and the next steps to be taken in the individual’s recovery
