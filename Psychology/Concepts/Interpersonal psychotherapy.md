> [!important] a time-limited (8–16 sessions)[[Therapy]]:
> -  individual therapy and group therapy
> - focuses on relationships
> - bi-directional links between emotions, symptoms and the [[stressor|stressful life event]]s
> - patients across the lifespan, from adolescence to late life (Cuijpers et al., 2016; Weissman, Markowitz & Klerman, 2018).

created by Gerald S. Klerman and Myrna M. Weissman
-  _IPT helps patients tolerate their emotions, which are experienced as a problem inside them, and use them to deal with problems outside them."_ —John Markowitz
## Psychological basis:
- the causes of [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] are multi-determined using a [[bio-psycho-social-cultural]] model
	- focus of IPT is on social:
		- Protective factors: social support, cultural rituals of coping
		- precipitating factors: loss, life changes, disagreements
- [[Mental Illness|mental health condition]]s arise from and affect interpersonal relationships

## Results:
- an increased sense of ==agency through interpersonal problem solving== (Weissman et al., 2018)
- [felt security and affiliation](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10879-007-9064-y) in relationships (Ravitz et al., 2008)
- increased reflective functioning (Markowitz et al., 2020)  -- learn from and within relationships
## Audiences:
- diagnosed disorder, e.g., **[[Depressive Disorders|depression]] , [[Bipolar Disorders|bipolar]] or binge [[Eating Disorders]] ** through [[Clinical interview]]
- no functionally impairing [[Substance Use Disorder]], cognitive impairment, acute [[Manic episode|mania]] or [[psychosis]] (IPT is not helpful)
### [[Rapport|Therapeutic Alliance]] 
### [[Trauma]]:
Traumatic experiences in childhood and adulthood are under-recognized and can manifest in **chronic mental health problems and struggles with helplessness, emotional dysregulation, shame or self-blame.**
- patients can be reluctant to trust [[therapeutic alliance]]

Beginning phase:
- ask if it's ok to talk about traumatic/frightening experiences in initial assessment

# Method:
## Phase1: **beginning**
discover temporal links between recent life events and symptoms to choose a therapeutic focus and IPT problem area
- [ ] Establish [[Rapport|Therapeutic Alliance]]
	- adverse childhood experiences (ACEs) & [[CARE method]] 
- [ ] Assess symptoms and link to [[stressor]]s
- [ ] [[psychoeducation|Psychoeducation]]
	- rights & responsibilities of the patients
		- [["sick mode" medical model]]
	- understand [[Depressive Disorders|depression]] [[Symptoms|symptoms]] -> alleviate blame/stigma
		![[Depressive Disorders#Symptoms]]
- [ ] 0.5 - 2 full sessions on [[Interpersonal Inventory]]
- [ ] Case formulation
	- case formulation uses [[bio-psycho-social-cultural]] model's factors ([[predisposing factor]], [[precipitating factor]], [[perpetuating factor]],[[protective factor]])
	- strengthen [[Rapport]] through agreement in focus of treatment
- [ ] Choose[[ IPT problem area]] and set goals
	- what psychosocial [[stressor]]s is associated w/ episode or worsened symptoms
		- most charged [[affect]] (==emotions==)
		- ==chronologically linked to symptoms==
	- which relationships are problematic
		_“You have been reflecting on your relationships and struggles over these past few sessions. What life events stand out for you as most closely linked to the beginning or worsening of your symptoms, and which relationships do you think will be most helpful to focus on as we move into the middle phase of our work together?”_
	![[IPT problem area]]
## Phase 2: **middle**
are to explore emotions and relationships
> [!NOTE] 
> therapist uses the emotionally charged material the patients bring in, and links symptom and mood changes to the interpersonal focus
- [ ] Track symptoms
- [ ] Work on [[IPT problem area]]
- [ ] Help patients connect to social [[Interpersonal Inventory|support system]] 
	- which people in [[Interpersonal Inventory]] helps to
		- reduce feelings of loneliness/sadness/shame
		- increase comfort with connection
	![[Interpersonal Inventory#^1b354d]]![[Interpersonal Inventory#^1a00b9]]
- [ ] Improve communication and interpersonal effectiveness
	- [[communication analysis]]
	- [[role play]]
- [ ] brainstorm problem solving
	- [[problem solving model]] 
	- [[decision analysis]] 
## Phase 3: **ending**
help patients prepare sustain recovery and review efforts, strategies and progress.
- [ ] Review and consolidate gains
- [ ] Explore feelings about termination
- [ ] Contingency plan
