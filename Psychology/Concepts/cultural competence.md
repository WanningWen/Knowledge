> [!important] the ability to work skillfully across cultures, engaging the perspectives and identities of both clinician and patient
>  “a set of congruent behaviors, attitudes, and policies that come together in a system, agency, or among professionals and enables that system, agency, or those professionals to work effectively in cross-cultural situations” 

#DOI  10.1007/s40596-016-0552-9
## Approach:
### structured [[Clinical interview]] vs crituqes
Ex: [[DSM V]] Cultural Formulation Interview, Outline for Cultural Formulation
- Pro: concrete tools
- Con:  rigidity & formulaic
Theoretical critiques:
- Con: few strategies of implementation
### content-oriented approach vs process-oriented approach
#### Content oriented:
- therapist learns cultural norms of patient's self-identified socialcultural group
	- ex:“like-ethnic” therapists: therapists of similar ethical background
Con:
- reductionistic: culture = ethnicity, language or country of origin 
- stereotypes assigned to patients
#### process-oriented:
- skill based:
	- focuses on how “clinicians ascribe meaning and integrate such meaning in the clinical encounter rather than what therapists know about specific groups”
- emphasizes attending to identity-based negotiations
	- including issues of power and perception
- more readily taught, measured, and then tested
Con:
- lack of specific directives

### the ==not-knowing stance==
#### 1. reflection
What to reflect: (metacognition - thinking about one's thinking)
- the cultures of patient, trainee, supervisor
- the practice of psychotherapy itself
Ways to:
- cultivating habits of mind such as experiencing information as novel
- ‘facts’ as conditional
- seeing situations from multiple perspectives
- suspending categorization and judgment
- self-questioning
Donald Schon: reflection in action (during therapy session) vs. on action ()
#### 2. humility: “a willingness and ability to listen and learn from patients”
How:
- address the social realities of the patient 
- to examine how the psychotherapeutic encounter enact power imbalances
- should [[Therapy]] be the method of healing or some other ways
Focus:
- patient readiness/commitment/psychological mindedness
#### 3. working with otherness
othering:
- when an individual or group is ascribed traits that are experienced as foreign, undesirable, or alienating.
[[self-concept]]
