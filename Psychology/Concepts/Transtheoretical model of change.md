> [!important] models of change developed by James Prochaska, and Carlo Diclemente in the 1970s
## Pre-contemplation stage: 
before you know there's something to change
=="nothing needs to change"==
### Traits:
- unaware, avoids thinking about, in denial/rationalization
- positive of the behavior outweigh costs or consequences of their behavior
## Contemplation stage
=="I'm considering change"==
consider the possibility of having a problem & change something about their behavior
	**ambivalence**: on the fence about wanting to make the change or not
### Traits:
- debating whether or not a behavior is a problem
- mix feelings
- BUT has not decided what to do about it
## Determination/Preparation stage
=="I'm figuring out how to change"==
**making a plan**: individual might work with treatment professional --> treatment plan
### Traits:
- ambivalence *no longer poses a total barrier*
- determination to make the change is present
- may be ready/getting ready to change behavior
- may take a long time to move to the next stage
## Action stage
=="I'm working on reaching my goals"==
### Traits:
- entering into treatment program, commitment
- steps and missteps
## Maintenance stage
"I've ==made my changes==, now I have to ==keep it up=="
### Traits:
- individual is no longer actively working to change their behavior
- integrated new behavior into their routine

> [!NOTE]  People who have become abstinent from drugs for 5 years are called *maintainers*
## Relapse/Termination stage
- norm that going through lapse and relapse of old behavior is the norm, not an indication of no hopes of lasting change
> Especially useful in addiction therapy
