> [!important] ==reflection==:  brief guess in the form of a statement 
# Why we need reflections
- check whether you understood the individual you're working with
- highlight individual's ambivalence
- steer towards greater recognition of their problems/concerns
- reinforce statements indicating the individual is thinking about  [[change talk]]
#### Examples
> [!quote] "I heard you say that on the one hand you'd like to sleep better at night, but on the other hand you're concerned about how that might affect your metabolism"

> [!quote] "sounds like"

> [!quote] "you're feeling like"

> [!quote] "from your point of view..."

## Primary [[Empathy]]  
> [!NOTE] Reflection of content and feelings  
### Purpose:  
5. To show that you’re understanding the speaker’s experience  
6. To allow the speaker to evaluate his/her feelings after hearing them expressed by someone else  
### Basic Formula:  
> [!NOTE] You feel (state feeling) because (state content)  
### e.g.  
- Student: I just don’t know how I am going to get all this math homework done before tonight’s game especially since I don’t get most of this stuff you taught us today.  
- Teacher: You are feeling frustrated and stuck…You are feeling frustrated and stuck with math you don’t know how to do and you’re worried that you won’t figure it out before you go to the game. 
- The main fear for you seems to be fear -- you’re really scared of losing your relationship if things don’t get better.  
- It’s upsetting when someone doesn’t let you tell your side of the story.  
## Advanced [[Empathy]]  
> [!NOTE] reflection of content and feeling at a deeper level.  
### Purpose: 
To try and get an understanding of what may be deeper feelings  
### e.g.  
- I get the sense that you are really angry about what was said, but I am wondering if you also feel a little hurt by it. 
- You said that you feel more confident about contacting employers, but I wonder if you also still feel a bit scared.
