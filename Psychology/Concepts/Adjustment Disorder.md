> [!info] occurs following an identifiable stressor that happened within the past 3 months:
>  a single event (loss of job, death of a family member) or a series of multiple stressors (cancer treatment, divorce/child custody issues).
## Clinical Presentation
> [!NOTE] does not have a set of specific symptoms an individual must meet for diagnosis
- stressor and must be significant
	- social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning and causes marked distress 
- “…that is out of proportion to the severity or intensity of the stressor” (APA, 2022, pg. 319).
### Modifiers:
 important when choosing treatment options for patients
- depressed mood, with anxiety, with mixed anxiety and depressed mood, with disturbance of conduct, with mixed disturbance of emotions and conduct, or unspecified
## Epidemiology/ Prevalence
- 50% of the admissions in the 1990s in psychiatric hospitals in the U.S., Australia, Canada, and Israel, adjustment disorders
- 5-20% of individuals in outpatient mental health treatment facilities have an adjustment disorder as their principal diagnosis.
### Gender:
- higher in women than men (APA, 2022)
## [[comorbidity]]
“adjustment disorders are common accompaniments of medical illness and may be the major psychological response to a medical condition” (APA, 2022).

Example:
- an individual with adjustment disorder with depressive mood must not meet the criteria for a major depressive episode
- the diagnosis of MDD should be made over adjustment disorder\
