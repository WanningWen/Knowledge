> [!info] phenomenon where there's occurrence of mental health disorders together

## Disorders:
### [[Depressive Disorders]]:
Nearly three-fourths of participants with lifetime [[MDD]] also met the criteria for at least one other [[DSM]] disorder (Kessler, Berglund, et al., 2003).
#### Cooccurrence in [[Major Depressive Disorder|MDD]]
- [[substance use disorders]] (Kessler, Berglund, et al., 2003)
- panic disorder, [[Anxiety Disorders]], [[Post Traumatic Stress Disorder|PTSD]], OCD, anorexia, bulimia, and borderline personality disorder
- women report comorbid anxiety disorders, bulimia, and somatoform disorders
- men report comorbid alcohol and substance abuse
#### Cooccurrence in [[Persistent depressive disorder]] (PDD)
- higher risk for psychiatric comorbidity
- for [[Anxiety Disorders]], [[Substance Use Disorder]], and personality disorders in particular (APA, 2022).
> [!question] researchers have tried to identify which disorder precipitated the other
-  the onset of depression is a direct result of the onset of another disorder (Gotlib & Hammen, 2009)
### [[Bipolar Disorders]]
Bipolar I disorder: 
- typically have a history of 3+ mental disorders:
- anxiety disorders, alcohol use disorder, other substance use disorder, and ADHD, borderline, schizotypal, and antisocial personality disorder
Bipolar II disorder:
- anxiety disorders being the most common (38% with social anxiety, 36% with specific phobia, and 30% having generalized anxiety)
- substance use disorders: alcohol use (42%), cannabis use (20%)
-  [[premenstrual dysphoric disorder]] in women
[[Cyclothymic Disorder]]:
- [[Substance Use Disorder]]
- sleep disorders
