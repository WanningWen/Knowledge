> [!important] (c. 1033 - 1109) Italian priest/Archbishop of Canterbury with [[Ontological Argument]] proving God's existence

![[Ontological Argument]]

## Belief in God:
- logically impossible to *have* an idea of God without God actually existing
- God is the only being where we can imagine it existing without it being in reality
> there's a lot of imaginary creatures

## Criticism against him
### perfect island counter:
Gaunilo of Marmoiutiers: you cannot conjure a perfect island into existence just by being able to imagine how it is like
### Reply:
- other things are only the most perfect of its kind, only God is the most perfect of *everything*
