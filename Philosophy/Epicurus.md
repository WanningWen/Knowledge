The point of philosophy is to make a better life. Epicurus believed that is to make living more intense
## Ideas
- philosophy should be practical
- the key to life was recognizing we all seek pleasure
- teaching a form of therapy:
	- physical pain could be made bearable by remembering past pleasures
opposite of the contemporary epicure ![[epicure]]
> [!NOTE] giving into greedy appetites would feed into more desire --> mental anguish of unfulfilled craving 

### Why shouldn't you fear death? 
> [!important] his epitaph
> "I was not; I have been; I am not; I do not mind"
#### 1. you won't experience it
Ludwig Wittgenstein echoed in Tractatus Logico Philosophicus: "death is not an event in life"
- death as a removal of the possibility of experience
> [!question] but people fear the dying process, not the moment of death?

Response to the **[[Ship of Theseus]]: a new body with all the same bits back together wouldn't be me despite looking like me. Once the body ceases to function nothing can bring it back to life
#### 2. thinking of the time after death similarly as which before birth
We don't usually worry about not existing for millennia before our birth
