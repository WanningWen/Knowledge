I want to write an email as an outreach to programs and research labs so that I can tell my story and try to get a role. Here's a cold emailing template that I want to work on:
  

Hi Dr. [name],


I hope this email finds you well. My name is Wanning "Marilyn" Wen, and I’d like to volunteer in your lab as a research assistant and a lived experience peer. I am an undergraduate majoring in Medicine, Health, and Society and Computer Science in Vanderbilt University. I have been heavily affected by Major Depressive Disorder, General Anxiety Disorder, and other trauma-related disorders during my college time. Coming from a background with close to no mental health education, I have decided to take a extended leave from college to manage my symptoms and pursue a change of career path towards a mental health profession. 

I am currently a Certified Medi-Cal Peer Support Specialist located in Mountain View, and I am passionate about utilizing my live experience to support research in psychology, particularly in benefiting those with similar cultural backgrounds as I.

I have extensive experience coding in R and python. I attached research papers I have previously worked on. In the past 6 months, I have been volunteering as a Ending the Silence Presenter for the National Alliance on Mental Illness in San Mateo. I am experienced describing and publicly speaking about my lived experience to others, including in auditoriums with 500+ people. I have also taken coursework in public health, biology, and chemistry. I'm bilingual in Mandarin Chinese and English. Although I haven't had experience in mental health research yet, I’m excited by the possibility of using my skills and experience to contribute to your lab in any way I can.

I’m looking to spend around 10-15 hours per week in the lab, over the next year. 

I’m interested in your research on [what the lab does]. The possible [applications/implications] of this research for [delivering care to the local xxx community through xxx] are so exciting!

If you or a member of your lab is open to discussing undergraduate research opportunities, I would be truly grateful for the chance to connect. I’m happy to meet when is best for you. While I'm sure there are many other students interested in research at your lab, but none may offer my dedication for better care delivery and my perspective as a peer in the community.  

Thank you so much for your time, and I look forward to hearing back from you!

  

  
Sincerely,
Wanning "Marilyn" Wen
