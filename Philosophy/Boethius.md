> [!important] 
> Ancius Manlius Severinus Boethius (475 - 525)

[[A Little History of Philosophy]]
One of the last Roman philosophers. ==Rome going downhill==. Similar to [[Cicero]] and [[Seneca]], used philosophy as a way of ==self-help/ therapy==

Christian and appealed to devoutly religious philosophers --> bridged from Greek and Roman to Christian philosophy

## Life:
- high office of Consul from King Theodoric (Goth who ruled Rome)
- accused of plotting against Theodoric, held in prison, tortured and executed
- wrote *The Consolation of Philosophy* in prison

## *The Consolation of Philosophy*
> [!NOTE] Boethius having a conversation with *Philosophy* in prison about luck and God 
### Luck 
Nature of luck is that it always changes
- Mortals are foolish to let their [[happiness]] depend on something so changeable
- nothing is terrible in itself - it all depends on how you think about it
- 
## [[Happiness]]  is a state of mind, not of the world
- riches, power, and honor are worthless - no one should base their happiness on something with such fragile foundations 
- true [[Happiness]] comes from belief in God or goodness 
	- God is not specifically the Christian God but based on Christian teaching about worthlessness of honor and riches and focus on pleasing God
## Learning
[[Plato]] believed learning is a recollection of ideas we already have - we just jogg memories of what we already knew
- Boethius should *already know* he shouldn't worry about his los of freedom and public respect
- his attitude to the situation matters because that's something he can choose

## God:
### Choice paradox
> [!NOTE]  if what God foresees must necessarily happen, then God knows how everything will turn out
> Is choice an illusion?
- even if it seems like different futures lie open for me when I make a decision
> [!NOTE] If we can't choose our actions, how is it fair to punish/reward us for what we do?
> challenges the premise of the [[Free Will Defense]]
#### Solution:
God's knowledge is not the same as predestination
- God is timeless (sees past, present and future at once) -- kind of similar to a parallel universe
