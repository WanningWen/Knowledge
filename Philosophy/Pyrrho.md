## Scepticism: philosophy of total indifference
> [!NOTE] extends on [[Socrates]] insistence that all he knew was how little he knew
- we can't completely trust the senses
	- Pyrrho didn't rule out they might be giving him accurate info but he kept an open mind
- conquer all usual and natural human emotions and patterns of behavior
- absolutely everything was a simple matter of opinion

> [!question] Three questions people seeking [[Happiness|happiness]] should ask:
> 1. What are things really like?
> 2. What attitude should we adopt to them?
> 3. What will happen to someone who does adopt that attitude?
1. We cannot ever know. This knowledge is not humanly possible (at odds with [[Plato]]'s theory of forms)
2. We should not commit to any view and suspend all judgement
	- Desires are belief that one thing is better than another. Unhappiness stems from unsatisfied desires 
3. One achieves tranquility after accepting nothing matters
