# Other people's inspirations:
## On Support system:
Part of growing a support system is being involved with enough people to go to so no one person feels overburdened. To do that you need to understand what triggers the need for support and what kind of support you want. Do you need a kind word? Encouragement? Just a smile and a thank you? A hug? Someone to vent to? Someone to give you a "get going kick in the pants"? The next step is looking for other places to find those things. How do you fill your time now? Is there somewhere that you could volunteer to help on your better days? Churches, senior center, food kitchens etc? When you are in a fishbowl and thee water becomes dirty you have two choices--put up with it or make change. Only you can change your life. It is very rare for positive change to just happen--the easiest first step is just writing your random thoughts on a piece of paper. This will help you connect the triggers with what response you would like. This can get better if you can find ways to use your better days to find a positive atmosphere and "bank" some good feelings about what you can add to others lives. Working on a GED is also a worthwhile goal. It too can help you feel better about yourself. Look into local programs to help with this.
