> [!important] one of England's greatest political thinkers (1588 - 1679)
## Thinkings
### Low trust in human nature
similar to [[Niccolò Machiavelli]]
- ALL human beings are selfish and driven by fear of death and hopes of personal gain
- All of us seek power over others
- only the ==rule of law and threat of punishment keeps us in check==
### "State of Nature":
> [!NOTE] a lawless world of scarce resources (food and water)
> It will be rational to kill others before they kill you
> life will be "solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short"

Problems:
- strongest could be killed at their most vulnerable; weakest could survive by cunningness
- co-operation would not exist without a basis of trust
- you would live on your own in fear of attack
### Solution: "social contract"
- putting a powerful individual/ parliament in charge 
- Hobbes believed laws are no good if there's no enforcing power
> [!NOTE] social contract
> giving up some personal freedom for safety

Hobbes thought safety was far more important than freedom:
- people would agree to form a social contract with each other and let sovereign impose laws because they are better off with authority in charge than fighting each other
> [!NOTE] "Sovereign"
> given the right to inflict severe punishment on anyone who stepped out of line
## *Leviathan* (1651)
Hobbes' most important book
- Leviathan is a gigantic sea monster in the Bible as a reference to the ==power of the state==
- opens with a giant made up of smaller people -- represent powerful state with a sovereign as its head
	- without sovereign society would decompose into separate people
### Politics:
- did not believe in democracy, did not believe people have abilities to make decisions for themselves
	- similar to [[Plato]]'s *The Republic*
- The state he described was [[Authoritarian]]
	- sovereign has almost unlimited power over citizens
## Fitness:
- Hobbes believed muscles and organs in the body are equivalent of clockwork mechanisms
## View on God:
- claimed that God must be a larger physical object
- refused to believe in existence of the soul
