## Philosophy:
> [!NOTE] *Meditations* and *Discourse on Method*
> Books exploring the limits of what he could possibly know
### [[Method of Cartesian Doubt]]
- Similar to [[Pyrrho]] Descartes did not like to believe anything without examining why
	- less extreme but he worked on if there ==anything he could know for certain== (if there is immunity to skepticism)
	- [[Pyrrho]]'s skepticism intent on showing nothing could be known for certain

### Idea of God:
Descartes argued that a good God must exist using [[Anselm]]'s [[Ontological Argument]] to argue that God wouldn't be perfect unless he was good

he produced:
#### [[The Trademark Argument]]
![[The Trademark Argument]]
- Because God is good --> God won't deceive humanity about the most basic experiences
- therefore, the world is more or less the way we experience it
- The world exists, more or less as it appears
