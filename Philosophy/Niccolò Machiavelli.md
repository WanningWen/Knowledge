> [!important] Italian thinker in city states (1467 - 1527)
> An effective prince has to "learn how not to be good" but *seem* honest and good
> The most important thing was to stay in power
> - Wrote *The Prince* (1532)
## *"The Prince"*
- Guidebook for those who had recently come into power
- inspired by Cesare Borgia (illegitimate son of Pope Alexander VI)
	- discovered the **Orsini** family planned to overthrow him, Borgia tricked them into believing he knew nothing and their leaders to talk to him at Sinigaglia, had them all murdered
	- put cruel commander **Remirro de Orco** in charge but distanced himself from de Orco
- Machiavelli believed that because Borgia's behavior produced desired effects and prevented further bloodshed, ==the ends justified the means== 
- stress that it's ==better to be feared than to be loved== as a leader
	- Machiavelli's cynicism of human nature -- human beings are unreliable, greedy and dishonest
	- it's dangerous to trust anyone to keep their promises unless they are terrified of the 违约consequences
- Effective leader should act like a beast
	- fox -- cunning; lion -- strong and terrifying
	- seeming to be honest and kind while breaking promises and acting cruelly
### *"Virtù"*
> [!NOTE] Italian for "manliness" or valour
- Machiavelli believed success depends on good luck & you can improve your odds by acting bravely and swiftly
- leader who prepares well and seizes the moment is more likely to do well than one who doesn't (==luck is preparation meets opportunity==)
