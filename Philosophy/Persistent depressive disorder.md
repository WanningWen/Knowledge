## Diagnosis:
an individual must experience a depressed mood ==for most of the day, for more days than not, for _at least two years==._ (APA, 2022)
accompanied by _two_ or more additional symptoms:
- changes in appetite
- [[insomnia]] or [[hypersomnia]]
- low energy or fatigue
- low self-esteem
- feelings of hopelessness
- poor concentration or difficulty with decision making
and these impacts can be as great as or greater than [[Major Depressive Disorder]]
> [!important] the individual will not be without symptoms for more than two months during this two-year period
### [[DSM V]]:
a blend of [[DSM]]-IV dysthymic disorder and chronic major depressive disorder.
- prevalence rate for [[DSM]]-IV dysthymic disorder is much lower than [[Major Depressive Disorder|MDD]] : ^05d574
	- 0.5% rate among adults in the United States
	- [[DSM]]-IV chronic major depressive disorder is 1.5%
	- prevalence among women is 1.5 and 2 times greater than the prevalence for men

## Treatment:
given the high rates of relapse and recurrences of depression following response to acute treatment, ==long-term continuation and maintenance therapy== are of great importance
- [[Continuation treatment]]s are defined as treatments given to currently remitted people (remission is defined as depressive symptoms dropping below case level) or to people who previously responded to an antidepressant treatment. 
- Maintenance therapy is given during [[recovery]] (which is defined as remission lasting longer than six months).
