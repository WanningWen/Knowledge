The Cartesian product of two sets A and B is another set, denoted as A × B and defined as $$A × B= (a, b) : a ∈ A, b ∈ B$$Thus A × B is a set of ordered pairs of elements from A and B
#### Real world example of Cartesian product:
The cartesian product of two sets A and B each denoting the outcome of Dice A and Dice B: $A × B = (a,b)$ is all the possible outcomes of rolling the two dice at the same time
## Rules of Cartesian products:
- Size: If A and B are finite sets, then $|A × B| = |A| · |B|$
