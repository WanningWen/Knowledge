# Definition #definition 
>[!info] The probability distribution of $X$ says how the total probability of 1 is distributed among (allocated to) the various possible $X$ values.
# Denotation $p(x)$ denotes the probability assigned to the value $x$ 
## **Probability Distribution and Probability Mass Function (PMF)**  
The **probability distribution** or **probability mass function (pmf)** of a discrete random variable (RV) is defined for every number $x$ by:  
$$
p(x) = P(X = x) = P(\{\omega \in \mathcal{S} : X(\omega) = x\}).
$$
In words, ==for every possible value $x$ of the random variable, the **pmf** specifies the probability of observing that value when the experiment is performed==.  
### conditions of PMF:
- Non-negativity:    $$
  p(x) \geq 0 \quad \text{for all } x.
  $$
- Total probability:    $$
  \sum_{\text{all possible } x} p(x) = 1.
  $$
