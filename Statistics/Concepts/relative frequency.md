If the experiment is performed $n$ times, the event $A$ will occur on some replications (i.e., the outcome will be in the set $A$), and on others, it will not.  

Let $n(A)$ denote the number of replications in which $A$ occurs. The ratio:
$$
\frac{n(A)}{n}
$$
is called the **relative frequency of occurrence** of the event $A$ in the sequence of $n$ replications.
