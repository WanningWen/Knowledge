# Definition

Let $X$ have a pmf $p(x)$ and expected value $\mu$.  

The **variance** of $X$, denoted by $V(X)$, $\sigma_X^2$, or simply $\sigma^2$, is defined as:  
$$
V(X) = \sum_{x \in D} (x - \mu)^2 \cdot p(x) = E[(X - \mu)^2].
$$  

---

### **Standard Deviation**  
The **standard deviation (SD)** of $X$ is the square root of the variance:  
$$
\sigma_X = \sqrt{\sigma_X^2}.
$$
