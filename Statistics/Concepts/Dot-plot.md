 a dotplot gives information about location, spread, extremes, and gaps.
### Example:data on the percentage of 25- to 34-year-olds in each state who had some type of postsecondary degree as of 2010
![[CleanShot 2025-02-19 at 13.27.57@2x.png]]
#### Description:
- There is clearly a great deal of state-to-state variability. 
- The largest value, for D.C., is obviously an extreme outlier, and four other values on the upper end of the data are candidates for mild outliers (MA, MN, NY, and ND).

## Cons of Dot-plot:
A dotplot can be quite cumbersome to construct and look crowded when the number of observations is large.
